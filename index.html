About Me

I am a researcher working in the field of Artificial Intelligence, with a focus on developing novel methods for image and video restoration. My research explores how advanced deep learning approaches—including diffusion models and GANs—can be applied to challenging imaging problems such as denoising, deblurring, super-resolution, and artifact reduction.

I am particularly interested in building generalizable AI models that work across diverse datasets and real-world conditions. My current work includes advancing state-of-the-art approaches for medical imaging (CT/MRI) while also extending them to broader applications like video restoration and low-light photography.

Beyond research, I enjoy collaborating on interdisciplinary projects, mentoring students, and exploring how AI can push the boundaries of human-centric computing.
