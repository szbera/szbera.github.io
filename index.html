About Me

I am a researcher working in the field of Artificial Intelligence, with a focus on developing novel methods for image and video restoration. My research explores how advanced deep learning approaches—including diffusion models and GANs—can be applied to challenging imaging problems such as denoising, deblurring, super-resolution, and artifact reduction.

I am currently working on video restoration and other multimedia projects, applying AI to improve the quality and reliability of modern imaging systems. Previously, my work focused on medical imaging (CT/MRI), where I developed deep learning models for low-dose CT denoising and artifact reduction.

Beyond research, I enjoy collaborating on interdisciplinary projects, mentoring students, and exploring how AI can push the boundaries of human-centric computing. Outside of work, I love travelling, playing cricket and football, and taking pictures.
